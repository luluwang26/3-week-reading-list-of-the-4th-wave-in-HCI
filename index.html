The Agentic HCI Reader

Research and practice on designing AI-native software — human-agent interaction, generative UI, and the trust problem — sequenced for a working product designer, not a doctoral seminar.

Before you start: in academic HCI, the “fourth wave” usually means Entanglement HCI — post-humanist theory about humans and technology as co-constituted. The applicable design work lives under different names: human-agent interaction, GenUI, agentic UX. This reader covers both threads; the sequence starts with the practical one.

Week 01

Orientation

Three pieces that establish the vocabulary: what the fourth wave actually claims, what the canonical guidelines already cover, and what changes when AI starts acting instead of suggesting.

  1. What is Human-AI Entanglement? A Guide to Fourth Wave HCI and Agential Realism

    Wendy Zhou · Essay Theory

    The most accessible primer on Barad’s agential realism applied to HCI: humans and AI as entangled, not separate entities that interact. Read it so the academic thread makes sense later — then set the theory down.

    start here — one sitting

  2. Guidelines for Human-AI Interaction & the HAX Toolkit

    Amershi et al. · CHI 2019 + Microsoft HAX Design Library Foundations

    Eighteen evidence-based guidelines synthesized from 150+ recommendations, organized by phase: initial interaction, in context, when the AI is wrong, and over time. The Design Library pairs each with patterns and shipped examples. Still the baseline every agent paper argues against or extends.

    browse the Design Library, don’t just read the paper

  3. Designing for Agentic AI: Practical UX Patterns for Control, Consent, and Accountability

    Victor Yocco · Smashing Magazine, Feb 2026 · 2-part series Trust & control

    The best practitioner piece on this list. Concrete patterns — the Autonomy Dial (progressive authorization: autonomy for low-stakes tasks, confirmation for high-stakes ones), intent previews, accountability frameworks. Autonomy is a technical output; trustworthiness is a design output.

    maps directly onto approval-policy thinking

Week 02

The research core

The three 2025–26 studies doing the heaviest lifting right now: agents in business contexts, interfaces generated at runtime, and what breaks when there’s more than one agent.

  1. Human-AI Agent Interaction in a Business Context

    arXiv, June 2026 Human-agent research

    Mixed-methods study — meta-analysis, participatory workshop, survey, interviews, conjoint experiment — building an empirically validated UX framework for business-critical agents. Pointedly notes that prior guidelines were validated on consumer products, not enterprise workflows.

    closest fit to AP/AR & fintech ops

  2. Generative and Malleable User Interfaces with Generative and Evolving Task-Driven Data Model

    Cao, Jiang & Xia · CHI 2025 Beyond chat

    The landmark GenUI paper. Instead of generating code, the system generates a task-driven data model that maps to UI — so end users reshape the interface through natural language and direct manipulation as their task evolves.

  3. Designing with Multi-Agent Generative AI: Insights from Industry Early Adopters

    DIS 2025 · Microsoft Multi-agent

    Key finding: the hard design problem isn’t individual agent performance but the coordination and comprehensibility of the collective system. Proposes a multi-agent UX toolkit — role-specific guidelines, transparency modules, visual debugging, autonomy-vs-control mechanisms.

Week 03

Depth & counterweight

Principles, skepticism, and governance. Two of these argue the interface itself is an oversight mechanism; one argues GenUI has debts nobody is pricing in.

  1. Design Principles for Human-Agent Interaction

    arXiv, 2026 Human-agent research

    A principles paper written specifically for agentic — not merely generative — systems. Read it against Amershi 2019 and note what had to change once the system takes actions.

  2. Hidden Technical Debt in Generative (GenUI) and Malleable User Interfaces

    arXiv, 2026 Beyond chat

    The skeptical counterweight to Week 2’s optimism: what breaks when interfaces are generated at runtime — consistency, testability, accountability, maintenance.

    read immediately after № 05

  3. On the Regulatory Potential of User Interfaces for AI Agent Governance

    arXiv, 2025 Trust & control

    Treats UI as a governance layer: visible thought and action traces as oversight, and the granularity tradeoff — too little detail undermines oversight, too much overwhelms the user.

  4. Human Control Is the Anchor, Not the Answer: Early Divergence of Oversight in Agentic AI Communities

    arXiv, 2026 Trust & control

    Against one-size-fits-all oversight: operational ecosystems need permission scoping, traceability, reversibility; social-facing ones need provenance and identity cues.

  1. Awesome Generative UI — Paper Index

After the sequence: the rest of the shelf

Sixteen further readings, kept in their themes. Pull from here when a project raises the question.

Theory & the paradigm shift

  • Entanglement HCI: The Next Wave? Frauenberger · ACM TOCHI 2019 The paper that named the fourth wave — our entanglement with technology challenges HCI’s foundations.
  • UX 3.0 Xu · 2024 UX 1.0 (PC) → 2.0 (mobile) → 3.0 (AI): ecosystem-wide experiences with explainability, controllability, and ethical alignment as first-class concerns.
  • Human-Centered AI Shneiderman · 2020 paper / 2022 book High automation and high human control are compatible — the foundation of every “oversight of agents” argument.

Canonical guidelines

  • Design Principles for Generative AI Applications Weisz et al. · CHI 2024 The generative-era update to Amershi: designing with variable, probabilistic output.
  • People + AI Guidebook Google PAIR Mental models, trust calibration, feedback, and errors — with practical worksheets.
  • Trust in Automation: Designing for Appropriate Reliance Lee & See · Human Factors, 2004 The classic. “Appropriate reliance,” not maximum trust, is the concept everything in the trust theme builds on.

Human-agent research

  • LLM-Based Human-Agent Collaboration and Interaction Systems: A Survey arXiv · 2025 A broad map of the collaboration-systems literature — useful for orienting and mining citations.
  • CowPilot: A Framework for Autonomous and Human-Agent Collaborative Web Navigation CMU · NAACL 2025 demo A concrete system exploring handoff between autonomous agent action and human takeover.
  • Human-AI-UI Interactions Across Modalities CHI 2026 workshop A snapshot of where the field is heading: agents that both generate UI and operate UI.

Beyond chat

  • The GenUI Study: Exploring the Design of Generative UI Tools to Support UX Practitioners and Beyond Chen, Knearem & Li · DIS 2025 What GenUI means for designers’ own workflows and role.
  • Beyond the Conversation Trap: Designing for Hybrid Human-Agent Interaction Modes designative.info · March 2026 Mixing conversational, visual, and direct-manipulation modes; visualization as an active reasoning component.

Multi-agent systems

  • Designing the Internet of Agents: A Framework for Trustworthy, Transparent, and Collaborative Human-Agent Interaction arXiv · 2025 A framework, SDK, and component library for agent-to-agent ecosystems with humans in the loop.

Trust & oversight

  • NN/g — State of UX & AI trust research Nielsen Norman Group · 2025–26 AI adoption is rising while trust is falling — trust as the central design problem. See also their work on “context architecture.”

Practitioner sources

  • Agentic Interfaces: How to Design AI-Native Products Beyond the Chat The Interactive Studio · 2026 Three in-product patterns: inline microinteractions, agent side panels with audit trails, and proactive nudges that silence themselves when ignored.
  • The Rulebook for Designing AI Experiences UX Collective · April 2026 A guided tour of HAX and Weisz’s principles — and how to actually put them to work.
  • UX Design Considerations for Human-AI Agent Interaction UC Berkeley CLTC Research project producing design recommendations on control loss and transparency in autonomous agents.

Concepts to track across everything

Appropriate reliance and trust calibration · progressive autonomy and autonomy dials · intent previews and plan editing · transparency granularity · reversibility and audit trails · the shift from designing screens to designing policies for agent behavior.
